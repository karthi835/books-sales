The Great Gatsby

By F. Scott Fitzgerald

Overview

Published: 1925

Genre: Tragedy, Historical Fiction

Summary

The Great Gatsby delves into the opulence and moral decay of the Roaring Twenties. Through the eyes of Nick Carraway, readers witness the enigmatic Jay Gatsby's pursuit of the American Dream, leading to tragic consequences.

Key Themes

  1. The American Dream
  2. Class and Social Status
  3. Love and Desire
  4. Morality and Decay

Did You Know?

F. Scott Fitzgerald's manuscript was rejected multiple times before publication. Today, it's one of the greatest American novels.

Critical Reception

Initially, The Great Gatsby received mixed reviews. Over time, it became a classic of American literature.
